Design Philosophy

My approach centers on maximizing creativity within limited space and resources by leveraging clever constraints and the right tools. While many creators strive for hyper-realism and intricate details, I lean toward a minimalist aesthetic infused with hidden mechanics and innovative, engaging interactions—unlocking the full potential of imagination within a space.

Smart Dorm — Intelligent Interactive Dormitory Series

Exploring highly functional automated geometric transformations within severely constrained spatial budgets.

Smart Dorm: The Starting Point of My Creator Journey in Rec Room

Earned: ₳ 1,571,500 Purchases: 2,245

As my first commercial release in Rec Room, this project explores spatial optimization and interactive design. I engineered the dorm with dynamic, space-saving mechanisms: a button-activated transforming bed, a wall-deployed TV, and retractable hidden stairs.

Officially featured by Rec Room, the project achieved thousands of sales in its first week. This explosive debut validated my design approach and laid the foundation for my signature Smart Dorm series.

Smart Dorm 2: Minimalist Aesthetic & Sleek Mechanics

Earned: ₳ 7,840,000 Deployments: 5,600

Leveraging my experience from the first project, I designed a second generation featuring a minimalist, modern aesthetic and a more seamless user experience. I introduced innovative mechanics to deliver a unique spatial experience, such as color-changing windows and an immersive tunnel-hovercar transition for player departures. This project ultimately became my best-selling and most successful creation to date.

Smart Dorm 3: Ultimate Geometric Deformation

Earned: ₳ 4,683,350 Deployments: 5,589

Smart Dorm 3 tackles the high-difficulty challenge of "dynamic folding mechanics." The design concept is "infinite micro-apartments." When the central control valve is clicked, secondary partition walls smoothly rotate and disappear via pistons, seamlessly expanding an independent living room into a double-story loft — all without consuming additional Ink budget.

sDorm: Extreme Reduction & VR Frame Rate Optimization

Earned: ₳ 1,567,650 Deployments: 900

The essence of design lies in "knowing when to stop." This minimalist space, optimized specifically for mobile VR devices, boldly strips away excess decoration. Polycount is compressed to one-fifth of the original, channeling resources into high-precision, zero-latency lighting feedback — proving that high-quality user experiences are achievable even under severe hardware constraints.

Smart Dorm 4: Modular Adaptability & Next-Gen Multi-Function

Earned: ₳ 311,000 Deployments: 450

As the culminating work of this series, Smart Dorm 4 explores the concept of "Modular Assembly." Users can freely adjust the coordinates of each functional unit (bathroom, workspace, media library) within the dormitory. The underlying programming automatically switches local network signal broadcast direction based on unit position, achieving high spatial adaptability.

Room: ^MasterBartender

Interaction: Multiplayer Online Physics Bartending Simulation | Global Visitors: 4,200+

"MasterBartender" is an immersive spatial proposal for virtual bar social scenarios. The design abandons traditional game panel-menu interactions in favor of highly physicalized gesture-based interaction. Players can physically grab cups at the bar, pour different chemical physics particles (simulating various cocktails), and trigger the Circuits V2 particle accelerometer through hand-shaking events to mix customized luminous liquids. This perfectly demonstrates the heights achievable when visual aesthetics, acoustic dynamics, and network variable synchronization are fused into a high-fluency virtual theater.

Creation Methodology

Analyzing how Rec Room's core spatial builder Maker Pen enables system development at the intersection of technology and design.

3D Spatial Modeling

Using the Maker Pen tool in 3D virtual environments, we can directly perform immersive modeling. Design primarily involves Boolean operations and proportional adjustments of primitive geometries (cubes, cylinders, spheres). To achieve barrier-free cross-platform experiences for mobile VR and PC, I focus intensely on optimizing the balance between vertex counts and lightmapping. Every Smart Dorm wall panel and MasterBartender bar corner is aligned at micrometer-level coordinates, combining sculptural aesthetics with system computational tolerance.

CORE: low-poly geometry & mesh efficiency

Event-Driven Circuit Logic (Circuits V2)

"Dynamics" are what give spaces life. Using the platform's underlying visual scripting language Circuits V2, I wire intricate logic control networks within 3D space. Through event listeners, asynchronous timer synchronization, and network-cached memory variables, all physical furniture is translated into "State-Synchronized Entities" — enabling multiple users operating the same bartending shaker or hidden bed within the same space to receive millisecond-level, zero-latency real-time feedback.

CORE: event-driven callbacks & sync algorithms
